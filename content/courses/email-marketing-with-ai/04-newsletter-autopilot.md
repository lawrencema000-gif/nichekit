# Monthly Newsletter on Autopilot

## One Email Per Month. AI Writes It.

A monthly newsletter keeps your brand in people's inboxes. The trick is making it useful enough that people actually open it, while spending as little time as possible creating it.

## The Template

Every month, same structure. AI fills it in.

```
Write my monthly newsletter for [MONTH] [YEAR]:

Store: [NAME]
Products: [WHAT YOU SELL]
Audience: Australians interested in [NICHE]

Structure:
1. SUBJECT LINE: Catchy, under 50 chars, curiosity-driven
2. INTRO (2 sentences): What's happening this month. Personal, warm.
3. TIP OF THE MONTH: One genuinely useful tip related to [NICHE]. 
   Something they can act on immediately. 100 words.
4. PRODUCT SPOTLIGHT: Feature one product. Why it's great right now 
   (seasonal, bestseller, new arrival). 50 words + link.
5. QUICK LINKS: 3 bullet points — latest blog post, social media, 
   any current promotions.
6. SIGN-OFF: Personal, Australian tone.

Keep the whole email under 300 words. People scan, they don't read essays.
```

## The Schedule

Set a monthly reminder. First Tuesday of every month:

1. **10 min:** Run the AI prompt with this month's details
2. **5 min:** Edit the output (add personal touches, check links)
3. **5 min:** Send via Resend or your email tool

**20 minutes per month.** That's it.

## Subject Lines That Get Opened

AI generates options, you pick the best:

```
Give me 10 subject line options for my [MONTH] newsletter. 
My store sells [PRODUCT] to Australians.

Mix of:
- Curiosity hooks ("The one thing we changed this month")
- Direct value ("3 ways to [benefit] this [season]")
- Personal ("Honest update from [store]")
- Timely ("[MONTH] special — you'll want to see this")

Under 50 characters each. No ALL CAPS. No excessive punctuation.
```

**Best performers for Australian audiences:**
- Questions: "Ever tried [thing]?"
- Numbers: "3 things we learned this month"
- Curiosity: "We almost didn't share this"
- Seasonal: "Your [season] sorted"

## Segmentation (When You Have 500+ Subscribers)

Once your list grows, send different content to different groups:

| Segment | What They Get | Why |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Bought in last 30 days | New product tips, loyalty reward | They're hot — keep them engaged |
| Bought 30-90 days ago | "Miss you" + featured product | Re-engagement |
| Never bought | Value content + occasional offer | Nurture toward first purchase |
| Email-only (never visited) | Your best blog post + product highlight | Give them a reason to click |

Start with one email to everyone. Add segments when you have enough data.

## Your Homework

1. Write your first newsletter using the template above
2. Send it to your current list (even if it's 5 people)
3. Set a monthly calendar reminder for the first Tuesday
4. Track open rates — aim for 30%+
