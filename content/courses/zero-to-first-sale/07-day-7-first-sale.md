# Day 7: Close Your First Sale

## Today: Make It Happen

You've done the work. Product is live. People know about it. Today is about following up and closing.

## Morning: Follow Up (30 min)

### Everyone Who Showed Interest But Didn't Buy

Go through your messages from Days 5-6. Anyone who said "cool!" or "looks great!" but didn't buy — follow up:

```
Hey! Thanks for checking out [store]. Did you have any questions? 
Happy to help if anything was unclear. The launch discount 
(20% off) ends tonight if you wanted to grab it.
```

### Everyone Who Visited But Didn't Respond

If you can see who clicked your link (check Vercel Analytics or your social media insights):

```
Hey! Saw you checked out [store]. Curious — was there anything 
that almost made you buy but didn't? I'm trying to improve 
and your feedback would be gold.
```

This either gets you a sale or incredibly valuable feedback. Win either way.

## Afternoon: One Last Push (30 min)

### The "Last Chance" Post

Post on social media:

```
Last chance — launch discount ends tonight.

[Product] — [one-line benefit]
20% off with code LAUNCH20

[link]

After tonight, it's full price. Thanks to everyone 
who's supported the launch this week.
```

### The Direct Close

If there's ONE person you think would genuinely benefit from your product, call them. Not text — call. Or voice message.

```
"Hey, I know I've been banging on about this, but I genuinely 
think [product] would help you with [their specific situation]. 
The discount expires tonight — just wanted to make sure you 
didn't miss it if you were interested."
```

This feels uncomfortable. Do it anyway. Sales is not about being pushy — it's about connecting someone with something that helps them.

## When the Sale Comes In

When you see that Stripe notification:

1. Screenshot it. Frame it. That's your first sale.
2. Send the customer a personal thank-you message (not automated — real)
3. Ask them: "What made you decide to buy?" (This insight is priceless)
4. Ask them: "Would you mind leaving a quick review?"

## What If It Doesn't Happen Today?

If you did everything in this course and didn't get a sale by Day 7:

**It's not a failure.** It's data. Ask yourself:
- Did 50+ people see my store? (If no, the problem is traffic, not the product)
- Did people click "Buy" but not finish checkout? (If yes, the problem is price or checkout friction)
- Did nobody click "Buy" at all? (If yes, the problem is the product page or the product itself)

Adjust and try again. Most successful stores didn't sell on Day 1. But the ones that never sold are the ones that stopped trying.
