# Scale This Store or Build Another

## You Have a Working Business — Now What?

You have a live store, real customers, and automated systems. This final module covers two paths: scaling this store bigger, or using what you've learned to build a second one.

## Path A: Scale This Store

### When to Scale

Scale when you have:
- Consistent sales (at least 1 per day)
- Positive customer feedback
- A clear understanding of who's buying and why
- Some profit after costs

If you're not there yet, go back to Module 8 and keep grinding the manual tactics.

### Scaling Lever 1: Paid Ads

Now that you have proven demand, ads make sense.

**Start with Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram):**

1. Install the Meta Pixel on your site (tell AI to add it)
2. Create a "Lookalike Audience" from your existing customers
3. Start with $10-20 AUD per day
4. Run for 7 days before judging results
5. Target: Australia only, age range of your typical customer

**The key metric: ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)**
- ROAS of 3x = you spend $1, make $3. Good.
- ROAS of 2x = break even after costs. Needs optimisation.
- ROAS under 1.5x = pause and rethink your ad or audience.

**Google Ads:**
- Target people searching for your product keywords
- Start with exact match keywords only
- Budget: $10-20/day
- Google Ads work better for products with clear search intent

### Scaling Lever 2: More Products

Add complementary products:
- **Bundles:** Package your best sellers together at a small discount
- **Upsells:** "Customers also bought..." on product pages
- **New products:** Ask your existing customers what else they want

AI helps here:

```
Based on my store selling [products] to [audience], suggest 
10 complementary products I could add. Consider:
- What my customers likely need next
- What pairs well with my existing products
- Price range $20-100 AUD
```

### Scaling Lever 3: Email Marketing

Your email list is your most valuable asset.

- **Weekly newsletter:** Tips, new products, behind-the-scenes
- **Flash sales:** 24-hour sales emailed to your list
- **Segmented campaigns:** Different emails for buyers vs. browsers

Email marketing returns $36 for every $1 spent on average. It's your highest-ROI channel.

### Scaling Lever 4: Partnerships

- **Influencers:** Find Australian micro-influencers (1K-10K followers) in your niche. Offer free product + commission on sales.
- **Complementary businesses:** Partner with stores that sell to the same audience but different products.
- **Affiliate program:** Let customers earn commission for referring friends.

## Path B: Build Another Store

### The Repeatable Playbook

You now have a proven system:

1. Pick a product (Module 2) — 2 hours
2. Set up the stack (Module 3) — 30 minutes
3. AI builds the store (Module 4) — 2 hours
4. Add products and pages (Module 5) — 2 hours
5. Configure payments (Module 6) — 30 minutes
6. Launch (Module 7) — 1 day
7. Get customers (Module 8) — ongoing
8. Automate (Module 9) — 1 day

**Total time to launch a new store: 1-2 weekends.**

### When to Build Another vs. Scale

Build another store when:
- Your current store is automated and profitable
- You've found another niche with clear demand
- You want to diversify your income streams
- Your current niche has a ceiling

Scale your current store when:
- You're not yet doing $5K/month
- Customers are asking for more products
- You have untapped marketing channels
- The niche is large enough to grow into

### The Portfolio Approach

Many successful e-commerce entrepreneurs run 3-5 small stores rather than one big one:

- Store 1: Digital templates for dentists ($2K/month)
- Store 2: Print-on-demand fitness gear ($1.5K/month)
- Store 3: Handmade candles ($3K/month)
- Store 4: Online course about [your expertise] ($1K/month)

Total: $7.5K/month. No single point of failure. All automated.
