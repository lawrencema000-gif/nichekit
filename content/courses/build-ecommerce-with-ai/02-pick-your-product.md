# Pick Your Product in 2 Hours

## Stop Overthinking This

The #1 reason people never start is they spend 6 months "researching the perfect product." Here's the truth: your first product probably won't be your best. That's fine. The goal is to pick something, launch it, learn from real customers, and iterate.

This module gives you a 2-hour process to go from "I have no idea what to sell" to "this is what I'm launching."

## The 4 Product Categories

Everything you can sell online fits into one of these:

### 1. Digital Products (Easiest to Start)
- Templates, guides, spreadsheets, checklists
- Online courses and tutorials
- Design assets, fonts, icons
- Software tools and plugins

**Pros:** Zero inventory, instant delivery, 90%+ margins, sell while you sleep.
**Cons:** Easier to copy, need to prove value before purchase.

**Best for beginners.** You can literally start today with $0.

### 2. Physical Products (Print-on-Demand)
- T-shirts, mugs, phone cases, posters
- You design it, a supplier prints and ships it
- Platforms: Printful, Printify, Gooten (all ship to Australia)

**Pros:** No inventory, no shipping headaches, low risk.
**Cons:** Lower margins (15-30%), shipping times can be slow to Aus.

### 3. Physical Products (Dropshipping)
- Source products from suppliers, sell at markup
- Supplier ships directly to your customer
- Popular suppliers: AliExpress, CJ Dropshipping, Spocket (for Aus/US suppliers)

**Pros:** Huge product selection, can test quickly.
**Cons:** Quality control issues, slow shipping from China, competitive.

### 4. Your Own Service (Highest Value)
- Consulting, coaching, freelancing
- Sell your time and expertise as packages
- Use the store as a booking/payment system

**Pros:** Highest margins, build relationships, hard to commoditise.
**Cons:** Doesn't scale as easily, limited by your time.

## The 2-Hour Product Selection Process

### Hour 1: Generate Ideas (30 min) + Filter (30 min)

**Step 1: AI Brainstorm (15 minutes)**

Open ChatGPT or Claude and paste this prompt:

```
I want to start an online store targeting Australian customers.
My interests/skills are: [LIST 3-5 THINGS YOU KNOW ABOUT]
My budget is: $0-100 to start
I want to sell: [digital products / physical products / services / not sure]

Give me 20 product ideas that:
- Have proven demand (people already buy this)
- Can be started with under $100
- Don't require specialist expertise
- Work well for the Australian market

For each idea, tell me: the product, target customer, estimated price point (AUD), and why it would work.
```

**Step 2: Quick Validation (15 minutes)**

For your top 5 ideas, do these 3 checks:

1. **Search it on Etsy/Gumroad/Amazon** — Are people already selling it? Good. That means demand exists. No results = no demand.
2. **Google it** — Search "[product] Australia" — Are there Australian businesses doing this? If yes, there's a market.
3. **Check the price** — What are people charging? Can you match or beat it while still making money?

**Step 3: Pick One (5 minutes)**

Choose the product that scores highest on:
- **You understand the customer** (you know what they need)
- **You can create/source it this week** (not next month)
- **People are already paying for it** (proven demand)
- **You can price it at $20+ AUD** (enough margin to matter)

Don't wait for certainty. Pick the best option and move.

### Hour 2: Define Your Offer (60 min)

**Step 4: Write Your Product Description (20 min)**

Use this AI prompt:

```
Write a product description for: [YOUR PRODUCT]
Target customer: [WHO BUYS THIS]
Price: $[PRICE] AUD
Key benefits (list 3-5):
- [BENEFIT 1]
- [BENEFIT 2]
- [BENEFIT 3]

Write it in a conversational, Australian tone. Not salesy, not corporate.
Include: headline, 2-3 paragraph description, bullet point features, and a clear call to action.
```

**Step 5: Set Your Price (10 min)**

Simple pricing formula:
- **Digital products:** Check competitors, price 10-20% below the market leader OR 20% above with a clear reason why.
- **Physical products:** Cost × 3 = your price. (If it costs $10 to source/make, sell for $30.)
- **Services:** Hourly rate × hours × 1.5 (the 1.5 accounts for admin, revisions, and your expertise).

**Step 6: Create a Simple Product Image (30 min)**

Use Canva (free) to create a product mockup:
- For digital products: Show it on a laptop/phone screen mockup
- For physical products: Use your supplier's photos or Canva mockups
- For services: Create a simple "what's included" graphic

## Australian-Specific Considerations

- **GST:** You don't need to register for GST until you earn $75,000/year. Under that, just price normally.
- **Consumer guarantees:** Under Australian Consumer Law, digital products must work as described. Physical products must be of acceptable quality. Don't overpromise.
- **Pricing:** Always display prices in AUD. Australians bounce when they see USD.
- **Shipping:** If physical, offer Australia Post for domestic. Sendle is cheaper for small parcels.

## Your Homework

By the end of this module, you should have:

1. ✅ **One product chosen** — written down, described, priced
2. ✅ **A product description** — AI-generated, refined by you
3. ✅ **A basic product image** — created in Canva
4. ✅ **A target price in AUD** — researched against competitors

Don't move to Module 3 until you have all four. No exceptions.
