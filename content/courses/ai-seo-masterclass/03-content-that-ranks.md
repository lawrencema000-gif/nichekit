# Write Content That Ranks (AI Does 90%)

## The Content Formula

Every piece of ranking content follows the same structure. AI writes it, you edit and publish.

## The SEO Blog Post Template

For every target keyword, use this prompt:

```
Write a 1500-word blog post optimised for the keyword: "[YOUR KEYWORD]"

Target audience: Australians looking for [your product/niche]

Structure:
1. H1 title (include keyword, under 60 chars)
2. Introduction (mention keyword naturally, hook the reader in 2 sentences)
3. 4-6 H2 sections that answer aspects of the search query
4. Practical advice in each section (not generic fluff)
5. Australian context (mention AUD, Australian cities, local references)
6. A conclusion with clear next step / CTA to your product
7. FAQ section with 3-4 questions (use "People Also Ask" from Google)

Tone: conversational Australian English. Like a knowledgeable mate 
explaining something — not a textbook.

Also provide:
- Meta title (under 60 characters, include keyword)
- Meta description (under 155 characters, include keyword, compelling)
- 3 internal link suggestions (to other pages on my store)
```

## The 80/20 Rule of SEO Content

**80% of your traffic will come from 20% of your posts.** You can't predict which ones. So publish consistently and let Google decide.

Minimum: **2 posts per month.** Ideal: **1 post per week.**

AI writes each post in 5-10 minutes. Your editing takes another 15-20 minutes. That's less than 30 minutes per post.

## Content Types That Rank

### 1. "Best [Product] in Australia" Posts
Search intent: someone ready to buy, comparing options.
Include your product alongside competitors. Be honest — readers and Google both reward transparency.

### 2. "How To" Guides
Search intent: someone learning, might buy later.
Teach something genuinely useful. Mention your product as a solution where natural.

### 3. "[Product] vs [Alternative]" Comparisons
Search intent: someone deciding between options.
Give a fair comparison. Win on your strengths without trashing competitors.

### 4. "[City] Guide" Posts
Search intent: local Australian searcher.
"Best Gifts in Sydney Under $50" or "Where to Find [Product] in Melbourne."

### 5. Seasonal Content
"Christmas Gift Guide for [Audience] (Australia)"
"Best [Product] for Australian Summer"
Publish 4-6 weeks before the season.

## On-Page SEO Checklist

For every page/post you publish:

- [ ] Keyword in the H1 title
- [ ] Keyword in the first 100 words
- [ ] Keyword in 1-2 H2 subheadings
- [ ] Keyword in the meta title and description
- [ ] Alt text on images (include keyword where natural)
- [ ] Internal links to 2-3 other pages on your site
- [ ] External links to 1-2 authoritative sources
- [ ] Content is 1000+ words (longer = better for competitive keywords)
- [ ] Genuinely useful — would you read this if you found it on Google?

## Editing AI Content (The Critical 10%)

AI writes 90% of the content. Your job is the 10% that makes it human:

1. **Read it out loud.** If any sentence sounds robotic, rewrite it.
2. **Add personal experience.** "In my experience..." or "What I've found with Australian customers is..." AI can't do this.
3. **Remove filler.** AI loves phrases like "In today's digital landscape" and "It's worth noting that." Delete all of these.
4. **Check facts.** AI occasionally makes up statistics. Verify any numbers.
5. **Add Australian flavour.** Spell check for Australian English. Reference Australian brands, cities, and culture where relevant.
