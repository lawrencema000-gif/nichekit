# AI Keyword Research for Australians

## Find What Australians Actually Search For

Keyword research is figuring out the exact words people type into Google. Get this right and everything else is easier.

## The AI Keyword Research Process

### Step 1: Seed Keywords (5 min)

Ask AI for a starting list:

```
I sell [YOUR PRODUCT] to Australian customers.
Give me 30 keyword ideas that Australians might search 
on Google when looking for this type of product.

Include:
- Product keywords ("buy [product] australia")
- Problem keywords ("how to [solve problem]")
- Comparison keywords ("[product] vs [alternative]")
- Local keywords ("[product] [city]")
- Question keywords ("is [product] worth it")

Format: keyword | estimated search intent (buy/research/compare)
```

### Step 2: Validate With Free Tools (15 min)

For each keyword, check:

**Google Autocomplete:** Type the keyword into Google. What does it suggest? Those suggestions are real searches.

**Google "People Also Ask":** Search your keyword. The "People also ask" box shows related questions real people ask. Each one is a content idea.

**Google Keyword Planner (free):** Sign up for Google Ads (you don't need to run ads). Use the Keyword Planner tool to see monthly search volume for each keyword.

### Step 3: Score and Prioritise (10 min)

Ask AI to score your keywords:

```
Here are my keywords. For each one, estimate:
1. Search volume (high/medium/low for Australia)
2. Competition (high/medium/low)
3. Purchase intent (high/medium/low)
4. Recommended action (target now / target later / skip)

Keywords:
[PASTE YOUR LIST]

Prioritise: low competition + high purchase intent = target now
```

### Step 4: Map Keywords to Pages (10 min)

Every keyword needs a page. Map them:

| Keyword | Page Type | URL |
|---------|----------|-----|
| buy [product] australia | Product page | /products/[product] |
| [product] vs [alternative] | Blog post | /blog/[product]-vs-[alternative] |
| how to [use product] | Blog post | /blog/how-to-[use-product] |
| best [product] in [city] | Local landing page | /[city]/[product] |
| [product] reviews australia | Blog post | /blog/[product]-reviews |

## Australian-Specific Keywords to Target

These patterns work especially well:

- **"[product] Australia"** — high intent, moderate competition
- **"[product] Melbourne/Sydney/Brisbane"** — low competition, high intent
- **"best [product] Australia [year]"** — time-sensitive, people want current info
- **"[product] free shipping Australia"** — buyers ready to purchase
- **"Australian made [product]"** — if applicable, huge trust signal

## The Long-Tail Advantage

Short keywords ("candles") are impossible to rank for. Long-tail keywords ("soy candles melbourne free shipping") are much easier and convert better.

**AI excels at finding long-tail keywords:**

```
Give me 20 long-tail keyword variations for "[YOUR MAIN KEYWORD]" 
that an Australian customer might search. Each should be 4+ words 
and specific enough that someone searching it is ready to buy.
```

## Your Keyword Spreadsheet

Create a spreadsheet with these columns:

| Keyword | Volume | Competition | Intent | Target Page | Status |
|---------|--------|-------------|--------|-------------|--------|
| | | | | | Not started / Written / Published / Ranking |

This becomes your SEO roadmap. Work through it systematically — one keyword per week minimum.
