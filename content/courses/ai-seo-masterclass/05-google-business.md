# Google Business Profile Masterclass

## Your Most Underrated Marketing Tool

Google Business Profile (GBP) is free, takes 30 minutes to optimise, and can drive more local traffic than months of blog posts. Most Australian businesses set it up once and never touch it again. That's your advantage.

## The Optimisation Checklist

### Photos (Most Impactful, Most Ignored)

Google ranks businesses with photos significantly higher. Upload:

- **Cover photo:** Your best product shot or storefront
- **Logo:** Clean, recognisable
- **Product photos:** Every product you sell (yes, all of them)
- **Workspace/team photos:** Behind the scenes, shows you're real
- **Customer photos:** With permission, showing products in use

**Minimum: 10 photos. Ideal: 25+.** Add 2-3 new photos per month.

### Business Description (250 Words)

```
Write a Google Business Profile description for my Australian 
online store:

Business: [NAME]
Products: [WHAT YOU SELL]
Location: [CITY/STATE]
USP: [WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT]

Include these keywords naturally: [YOUR TOP 3 KEYWORDS]
Write 250 words. Conversational, not corporate.
Mention we're Australian owned and operated.
End with a call to action to visit the website.
```

### Products/Services Section

Add every product individually with:
- Product name
- Price (in AUD)
- Photo
- Short description (1-2 sentences)
- Link to the product page on your website

This makes your GBP profile a mini storefront.

### Q&A Section

Pre-populate with common questions (ask them yourself, then answer):

1. "Do you ship Australia-wide?" → "Yes! We offer standard ($9.95) and express ($14.95) shipping across Australia."
2. "Do you accept Afterpay?" → "Yes, we accept Afterpay, as well as Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Apple Pay, and Google Pay."
3. "What's your returns policy?" → "We offer 30-day returns on all products. See our website for full details."

## Weekly GBP Posts (5 Minutes)

Post every week. Reuse your social media content but shorter:

**Types of posts:**
- **What's new:** New product announcements
- **Offer:** Discount codes or sales
- **Event:** Markets you're attending, seasonal promotions
- **Update:** Behind-the-scenes, business milestones

Always include a CTA button (Shop Now, Learn More, Call Now).

## Responding to Reviews

### Positive Reviews
Reply within 24 hours. Be personal:

```
Thanks so much, [Name]! Really glad you're loving the [product]. 
Your support means the world to our little Aussie business. 
See you next time!
```

### Negative Reviews
Don't panic. Don't get defensive. Reply publicly, then resolve privately:

```
Hi [Name], really sorry to hear about your experience. 
That's not the standard we aim for. I've sent you a 
direct message to sort this out — want to make it right.
```

Then DM/email them to resolve. Often they'll update their review.

### No Reviews Yet?
That's normal for new businesses. Focus on getting your first 5 reviews — the difference between 0 and 5 reviews is massive in the map pack.

## Tracking GBP Performance

In your GBP dashboard, check monthly:
- **Views:** How many people saw your profile
- **Searches:** What keywords triggered your listing
- **Actions:** Clicks to website, direction requests, phone calls
- **Photo views:** Which products get the most attention

This is free analytics that tells you exactly what your local audience wants.

## Your Homework

1. Optimise every field in your GBP
2. Upload 10+ photos
3. Add all products to the Products section
4. Pre-populate 3 Q&As
5. Post your first GBP update
6. Ask your 3 most recent customers for a Google review
