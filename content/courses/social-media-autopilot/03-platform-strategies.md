# Platform-Specific Strategies

## Each Platform Has Different Rules

What works on Instagram doesn't work on TikTok. This module breaks down the strategy for each platform so you're not wasting posts.

## Instagram

### What Works
- **Carousels** (swipe posts) get 2x the engagement of single images
- **Reels** (short video) get pushed by the algorithm more than photos
- **Stories** (daily) keep you visible without cluttering your grid

### Strategy for Stores
- Post 4-5 times per week to feed
- Story every day (even just a quick behind-the-scenes snap)
- Use all 30 hashtags (put them in the first comment, not the caption)
- Engage with similar accounts for 10 min after posting (algorithm boost)

### AI Prompt for Instagram Carousels
```
Create a 5-slide Instagram carousel about [TOPIC]:
Slide 1: Bold hook headline (stop the scroll)
Slide 2-4: One tip per slide (short, punchy, visual-friendly text)
Slide 5: CTA — "Save this for later" + "Follow @[handle] for more"

Keep text on each slide under 30 words.
```

## TikTok

### What Works
- **Hook in first 3 seconds** or people scroll past
- **Trending sounds** boost visibility massively
- **Raw, unpolished content** outperforms professional videos
- **Storytelling** keeps watch time high (the #1 ranking factor)

### Strategy for Stores
- Post 3-4 times per week
- Film on your phone — authenticity beats production quality
- Use trending sounds (even for product showcases)
- Show the product in use, not just the product

### AI Prompt for TikTok Scripts
```
Write a TikTok script (60 seconds) for my product [PRODUCT]:

Hook (first 3 seconds): Something that makes them stop scrolling
Problem (10 sec): The frustration my customer has
Solution (15 sec): How my product fixes it
Proof (15 sec): Quick demo or result
CTA (5 sec): "Link in bio" or "Comment [word] for the link"

Tone: conversational, like talking to a friend. 
Not scripted-sounding. Australian.
```

## Facebook

### What Works
- **Groups** are more powerful than Pages for small businesses
- **Long-form text** posts get more engagement than link posts
- **Facebook Marketplace** is underused for e-commerce
- **Community building** beats broadcasting

### Strategy for Stores
- Post 3-4 times per week to your page
- Join 3-5 relevant Australian groups and be genuinely helpful
- Share occasionally in groups (follow each group's rules)
- Use Facebook Marketplace for physical products

## LinkedIn

### What Works
- **Text-only posts** often outperform posts with images
- **Personal stories** about your business journey perform best
- **Professional tips** related to your niche
- **No hashtags needed** (LinkedIn's algorithm doesn't weight them much)

### Strategy for Stores
- Post 2-3 times per week
- Focus on business lessons, not product promotions
- Share wins and failures honestly
- Connect with other Australian business owners

### AI Prompt for LinkedIn
```
Write a LinkedIn post about [BUSINESS LESSON/STORY]:

Format:
- Opening line (hook — make them click "see more")
- 3-5 short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each)
- End with a question to encourage comments
- No hashtags

Tone: professional but human. Not "thought leader" cringe.
Share a real insight, not a humble brag.
```

## The 2-Platform Rule

Don't try to be everywhere. Pick 2 platforms and do them well:

| If your product is... | Choose these 2 |
|----------------------|----------------|
| Visual (fashion, food, beauty) | Instagram + TikTok |
| B2B or professional | LinkedIn + Instagram |
| Local service | Facebook + Instagram |
| Digital product | Twitter/X + LinkedIn |

Master 2 before adding a third.
